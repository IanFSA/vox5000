Help & Guide

Everything you need to get the best out of Vox5000.

Solo Recording

The home page recorder is for recording yourself only — no guests, no internet connection needed beyond loading the page.

  • Select your microphone from the dropdown
  • Choose your format — MP3 for smaller files, WAV for broadcast quality masters
  • Do a 5 second mic test before every session to check your levels
  • Keep the level meter peaking around –12 dB — never hitting 0 dB (red)
  • Hit Record — the button also stops the recording when pressed again
  • Your file downloads automatically when you stop — nothing is sent to any server

Interview Rooms

Interview rooms let you record remote interviews with up to 3 guests. Everyone records locally on their own device for the best quality.

Host Click "Start Interview Room" on the home page. Enter your name. Your room is created with a unique code.

  • Copy the invite link and send it to your guests via WhatsApp, email or any messaging app
  • Guests appear in a waiting queue — admit or deny each one
  • You can see each guest's consent status — green tick means consented, eye icon means Observer
  • Hit Record — a 5 second countdown plays on all screens, then recording begins automatically
  • Use the Mark buttons during recording to drop Intro, Break, Outro and Clip timestamps
  • Hit Stop — guests automatically send their audio to you
  • Download each person's track separately, clearly labelled with their name
  • Download the consent log — keep it as legal proof

Guest Click the link the host sent you. No account or download needed.

  • Put headphones on before joining — speakers cause echo that ruins the recording
  • Enter your name — it will be used to label your audio track
  • Tick the consent checkbox if you agree to be recorded. If you don't tick it, you join as an Observer — you can hear and chat but won't be recorded
  • Wait for the host to admit you
  • When the host starts recording you'll see a countdown, then a red recording indicator
  • When the host stops, your audio uploads automatically — keep the page open until it says Done

Markers

Use the Intro, Break, Outro and Clip buttons during recording to drop timestamp markers. When you stop, a text file downloads with all your markers listed with their timestamps — open it alongside your audio in Audacity to navigate quickly.

Input Gain & Monitor

The Input Gain knob boosts or cuts your microphone signal before it hits the recorder. Drag it up to increase, down to decrease. Double-click to reset to 0 dB.

The Monitor knob controls how loud you hear yourself in your headphones. It does not affect the recording — only what you hear. Double-click to reset.

My Rooms

Rooms persist in your browser until you delete them. Use the My Rooms page to see all your rooms, open them, copy invite links, rename them, or delete them.

If you return to a room you created, you'll go straight in without needing to re-enter your name.

Best practices for broadcast quality

  • Always use wired headphones — Bluetooth introduces latency and potential dropouts
  • Use an external USB or XLR interface microphone — not your laptop's built-in mic
  • Record in a quiet room — close doors and windows, turn off air conditioning
  • Do the 5 second mic test before every session
  • Keep levels peaking at around –12 dB — if the meter goes red you're clipping
  • Use Chrome on desktop for the most reliable experience
  • On mobile, use Chrome — Safari has limited Web Audio API support

Privacy & data

All audio recorded in solo mode stays on your device. Nothing is sent to any server. In interview rooms, guest audio is transferred directly from their browser to yours — peer to peer, not through any central server.

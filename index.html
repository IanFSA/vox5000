Why this exists

Built because the alternatives were not good enough.

When remote interviews became part of the daily workflow, the tools available were either too expensive, too complex, or simply not built for broadcast.

Most tools compress audio and deliver a single mixed track. Others cost money every month and assume you want a full video production studio.

Vox5000 was built by a working broadcaster who needed clean separate tracks, local files, and a guest link that just works without a subscription, an account, or an install.

That is still all it is. That is the point.

What makes it different.

Four things that matter to anyone who records for a living.

01

Your audio never touches a server

Most tools upload your audio to the cloud. Vox5000 does not. Solo recordings stay on your device. Interview tracks transfer directly between participants, peer to peer.

Separate tracks as standard

A single mixed track is useless in post production. Vox5000 gives you one clean file per speaker, labelled by name, from the moment you hit stop.

03

The level meter, gain control, mic test, markers, and format options were designed by someone who records for air. Practical decisions, not decorative ones.

04

Free with no strings

No trial. No freemium limit. No credit card. No tier that hides the useful features. Free because it was built to be used, not to convert you.

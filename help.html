Quick start

Recording yourself: Click "Record Myself" on the home page → complete the preflight checklist → select your mic → do a 5s test → hit Record.

Recording an interview: Click "Start Interview Room" → enter your name → copy the invite link → send it to your guest → admit them when they appear → hit Record → hit Stop when done → download all tracks.

Solo recording

The solo recorder is for recording yourself only — no guests, no internet connection required beyond loading the page.

  • Select your microphone from the dropdown
  • Choose MP3 for smaller files or WAV for a broadcast quality master
  • Do a 5 second mic test before every session to check your levels
  • Keep the level meter peaking around –12 dB — never hitting 0 dB
  • Hit Record — the same button stops the recording when pressed again
  • Your file downloads automatically when you stop — nothing is sent to any server

Interview rooms — host guide

HostYou create and control the room.

  • Click "Start Interview Room" from the home page
  • Enter your name — it labels your audio track
  • Copy the invite link and send it to guests via WhatsApp, email or any messaging app
  • Guests appear in a waiting queue on your screen — admit or deny each one
  • You can see each guest's consent status: 🟢 Consented, 👁 Observer, or ⚠ Not consented
  • Hit Record — a 5 second countdown plays on all screens, then recording begins on all devices simultaneously
  • Use the Mark buttons to drop Intro, Break, Outro and Clip timestamps during recording
  • Hit Stop — a 5 second warning appears, then guests automatically send their audio to you
  • Keep the page open until all tracks show a green tick
  • Download each person's WAV track, clearly labelled with their name
  • Download the consent log — keep it for your records

Muting and removing guests: Each guest row has a Mute button and a Kick button. Muted guests can still hear but their mic is silenced. Kicked guests are removed and shown a thank you message.

Room persistence: Rooms are saved in your browser. If you navigate away and return to the same room URL, you go straight back in without re-entering your name.

Guest guide

GuestClick the link your host sent you. No account or download needed.

  • Put headphones on first — speakers cause echo that ruins the recording for everyone
  • Enter your name — it will label your audio track in the final download
  • Tick the consent checkbox if you agree to be recorded. If you don't tick it, you join as an Observer — you can hear and chat but your mic will be off and you won't be recorded
  • Wait for the host to admit you
  • When the host starts recording you'll see a 5 second countdown, then a red recording indicator appears
  • Speak normally — your audio is recorded locally on your device
  • When the host stops recording, your track uploads automatically to the host
  • Keep the page open until you see the green Done message — closing it early will lose your track
  • After Done appears you can close the page

Markers

Use the Intro, Break, Outro and Clip buttons during recording to drop timestamp markers in real time. When you stop, a text file downloads alongside your audio with all markers listed. Open it in Audacity or your DAW to navigate quickly to key moments.

Input Gain & Monitor

Input Gain — boosts or cuts your microphone signal before it hits the recorder. Drag the knob up to increase, down to decrease. Double-click the knob or the dB number to reset to 0 dB.

Monitor — controls how loud you hear yourself in your headphones. It does not affect the recording at all — only what you hear. Double-click to reset. Start at –60 dB (silent) and raise until comfortable.

The level meter — aim for peaks between –24 and –6 dB (the green zone). Above –6 dB the signal clips and distorts. Below –24 dB the recording will be too quiet.

Consent & privacy

Solo mode: Your audio is recorded entirely on your device. Nothing is sent to any server. Vox5000 never sees your audio.

Interview rooms: Each participant records locally on their own device. When the session ends, guest tracks are transferred directly from each guest's browser to the host's browser — peer to peer. Vox5000 does not receive, store or process any audio.

Consent log: When a guest enters a room, their name, consent decision, timestamp, IP address and browser details are recorded and stored locally in the host's session. This data never goes to Vox5000's servers. The host can download the consent log as a text file to keep for their own records. The log documents who consented to recording and who joined as an Observer.

Observer mode: If a guest does not tick the consent checkbox, they join as an Observer. Their microphone is switched off and they cannot be recorded. They can still hear everyone and use the text chat.

Best settings for broadcast quality

  • Browser: Google Chrome on desktop or laptop — most reliable
  • Headphones: Wired — Bluetooth adds latency and potential dropouts
  • Microphone: External USB or XLR interface mic — not your laptop's built-in mic
  • Environment: Quiet room, doors closed, air conditioning off
  • Level: Peaks at –12 dB on the meter — never hitting 0 dB red
  • Format for broadcast: WAV 48kHz 24-bit for masters, MP3 320kbps for delivery
  • Mobile: Chrome on Android is acceptable. iPhone Safari is not recommended for recording
  • Long sessions: Keep your screen on and don't switch apps — especially on mobile

Fix common problems

🚫 My microphone is not showing up
Make sure your mic is plugged in before loading the page. If it's a USB mic or interface, check it appears in your computer's sound settings. Refresh the page after plugging it in. If you're on Windows, check that Chrome has mic permission in Windows Settings → Privacy → Microphone.
🔒 I clicked Deny on the microphone permission
Click the padlock or microphone icon in Chrome's address bar → Site settings → Microphone → Change to Allow. Then refresh the page.
🔊 I can hear myself echoing
You or your guest is not using headphones. Speakers pick up the audio from the call and feed it back into the mic. Everyone in an interview room must use headphones or earphones.
📊 The recording is too quiet or distorted
Too quiet — increase the Input Gain knob until peaks hit around –12 dB. Distorted (red on waveform) — decrease Input Gain immediately. Aim for peaks between –24 and –6 dB.
👥 The guest is not appearing in my waiting queue
Make sure you sent them the correct link — it should contain your room code (e.g. room.html?r=XXXXXX). Both you and the guest need to be on the same page at the same time. Ask them to refresh and try again. Both should use Chrome on desktop if possible.
📤 The guest upload is stuck or not progressing
Both the host and guest must keep their page open and not lock their screen. If upload stalls, the guest should download their own track using the browser's download option before closing. Ask them to send it to you via WeTransfer or Google Drive as a fallback.
📱 I'm on an iPhone and recording isn't working
Safari on iOS has limited Web Audio API support. Use Chrome on iOS instead. For the most reliable experience, join from a desktop or laptop computer. Do not lock your screen or switch apps during recording on mobile.
💾 I can't find my downloaded file
Check your Downloads folder. On Chrome, press Ctrl+J (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+J (Mac) to see recent downloads. Files are named with your name and a timestamp, e.g. Ian_HOST_2026-06-19.wav
🏠 My rooms have disappeared
Rooms are stored in your browser's local storage. They can be lost if you clear your browser data, use private/incognito mode, or switch to a different browser or device. Always download your files before clearing browser data.
